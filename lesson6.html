LESSON 6 - FINAL PROJECT

Build a Complete News Article Page

Put everything together in a professional news article

Your Final Project

Congratulations! You've learned HTML and CSS fundamentals. Now it's time to build a complete, professional news article page that includes everything you've learned.

Project Requirements

Your news article page must include:

  1. Site Header with navigation menu
  2. Article headline, byline, and date
  3. Lead/lede paragraph (styled differently)
  4. At least 4 paragraphs of article content
  5. At least one image with proper caption and credit
  6. A subheading (h2 or h3)
  7. A pull quote
  8. A bulleted or numbered list
  9. Related articles sidebar or section
  10. Footer with copyright

Complete Template Code

Here's a complete, professional news article template you can use and customize:

How to Use This Template

STEP-BY-STEP INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Copy the entire code above into your text editor
  2. Save it as: final-article.html
  3. Customize the content:
    • Change the site name from "Campus News Daily"
    • Write your own article headline
    • Replace with your own article content
    • Add your own byline and date
    • Change the image (use Unsplash for free images)
  4. Experiment with the CSS:
    • Try different colors
    • Change fonts
    • Adjust spacing and sizes
  5. Test in your browser and make adjustments

Final Project Assignment

CREATE YOUR OWN NEWS ARTICLE

Choose one of these topics or create your own:

  • A major campus event or announcement
  • A profile of an interesting person in your community
  • An investigative piece on a local issue
  • Coverage of a sports event or competition
  • A feature story about a campus tradition or club

Your article must include:

  1. All structural elements (header, nav, article, footer)
  2. Proper HTML5 semantic tags
  3. Professional CSS styling
  4. At least one image with caption
  5. Responsive design (works on mobile)
  6. At least 300 words of original content
  7. Proper byline, dateline, and category
  8. Related articles section
Bonus Points: Add a second article page and link them together, create a homepage with multiple article links, or add social media sharing buttons!

Evaluation Checklist

Use this to check your work before submitting:

HTML Structure

  • ✓ Proper DOCTYPE declaration
  • ✓ Complete head section with title
  • ✓ Semantic HTML5 tags (header, nav, article, aside, footer)
  • ✓ All tags properly closed
  • ✓ Valid alt text on images

Content

  • ✓ Clear, engaging headline
  • ✓ Strong lead paragraph
  • ✓ Well-organized body content
  • ✓ Proper byline and date
  • ✓ At least one subheading
  • ✓ Image with proper caption and credit

CSS Styling

  • ✓ Professional color scheme
  • ✓ Readable typography
  • ✓ Proper spacing and layout
  • ✓ Consistent styling throughout
  • ✓ Hover effects on links
  • ✓ Responsive design (mobile-friendly)

Journalism Standards

  • ✓ Accurate, well-researched information
  • ✓ Proper attribution of sources
  • ✓ Photo credits included
  • ✓ Copyright notices where needed
  • ✓ Professional tone and writing
